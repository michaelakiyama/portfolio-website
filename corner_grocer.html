Corner Grocer

A responsive web app created using thorough market analysis, user discovery and user influenced designs.

My Process

A five phase, cyclical process that encompasses analyzing current market conditions, getting personal with potential users, generating ideas, actioning on those concepts and showcasing market ready product representations.

Understand - 5W's

Who, What, Where, When, Why

Environmentally conscious users need a convenient recipe search tool that suggests options based off of ingredients that are produced and sold locally so that they feel good about where their food comes from, how it was grown and who benefits from their patronage.

SWOT Analysis

To gain further insight into current market conditions, competitive research was conducted highlighting their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats.

Hypothesis

Users want a simple way to search through many options of affordable, easy to make recipes made from locally sourced products.

User Interviews

In this phase I started to test my hypothesis by collecting input from potential users by hosting live interviews and translating their feedback into user personas.

Userflow

I utilized the feedback I received from the previous phase to begin mapping out a user's path from the beginning of a task to its completion.

Low-Fi Wireframes

Using the Crazy 8 method, low-fidelity wireframes were quickly created in order to allow ideas to flow freely. Even at this early stage, user testing was possible with a rough prototype with the goal of evaluating potential design elements and flow logic.

Styleguide

I put testing aside for a moment to focus on product aesthetics and set visual boundaries explained through the following style guide.

Wireframe Progress

Remember the Crazy 8 wireframes? I used them to progress to mid-fidelity versions and the style guide to move into high fidelity models.

User Testing

I was having trouble deciding on how to display recipe search results, arguably one of the most important screens. So I turned to users to make that decision.

Mockup

With user feedback in hand, it was time make corrections, apply final designs to screens at all breakpoints and showcase them through mockups.

Next Steps

Additional testing and user interviews are needed to prepare for the next iteration. Are user needs being met? How easy are tasks accomplished? What features are missing? Is their still a competitive edge? These are just a few questions to be answered in the following stage of development.
